# Trace — Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 2026-02-19

Trace is built to be **privacy-first** and **local-only**.

## Summary (plain English)
- Trace stores your data **only on your Mac**
- Trace does **not** send your screen content to any server
- Trace does **not** sell or share your data
- You control permissions and can stop capture anytime

---

## What Trace Collects (on-device only)

Trace may process and store the following data locally to provide search and recall features:

- Screen captures (images of what was visible on your screen)
- Extracted text (OCR)
- App metadata (e.g., active app name / bundle id, window title if available)
- Timestamps and session/context metadata
- Embedding vectors used for semantic search

All of the above stays **on your device**.

---

## What Trace Does NOT Collect
- No account creation
- No cloud storage
- No analytics tracking
- No advertising identifiers
- No sharing/selling of your data
- No remote access

---

## Permissions

### Screen Recording
Trace requires **Screen Recording** permission to capture your screen for local indexing and search.

You can control this at any time:
**System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording**

If disabled, Trace cannot capture new items.

---

## Data Storage
Trace stores data inside the macOS app container on your device (Application Support).  
Data may include a local database and cached thumbnails.

---

## Data Deletion
You can remove Trace’s stored data by:
- Deleting the app, and/or
- Removing its container data (advanced users)

(You should only do this if you understand what you’re deleting.)

---

## Children’s Privacy
Trace is not designed for use by children and does not knowingly collect personal data.

---

## Changes
If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will be updated.
